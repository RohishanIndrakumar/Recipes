Briyani

Instructions:
1. Marinate the chicken with yogurt, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Let it marinate for at least 1-2 hours.
2 In a large pot, heat oil or ghee. Add sliced onions and fry until golden brown. Remove half of the fried onions for garnishing.
3 Add ginger-garlic paste to the remaining onions in the pot and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
4 Add chopped tomatoes, biryani masala, and green chilies. Cook until the tomatoes are soft.
5 Add marinated chicken to the pot and cook until the chicken is partially cooked.
6 Meanwhile, parboil the soaked basmati rice with some whole spices until it's 70-80% cooked. Drain the water.
7 Layer the partially cooked chicken with mint leaves, coriander leaves, and half of the fried onions.
8 Top the chicken layer with the parboiled rice. Sprinkle some biryani masala, mint leaves, coriander leaves, and the remaining fried onions.
9 Mix saffron strands in warm milk and drizzle it over the rice for a fragrant and colorful touch.
10 Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid or seal it with dough to trap the steam.
11 Cook on low heat for about 25-30 minutes or until the chicken is fully cooked, and the rice is tender.
12 Gently fluff the biryani with a fork, ensuring not to break the rice grains.
13 Serve the flavorful Hyderabad Chicken Biryani hot with raita or a side salad. Enjoy your delicious homemade biryani!

    ingridients :
  • 1 kg chicken, cleaned and cut into pieces
  • 1 cup yogur
  • 4 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 4 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup cooking oil or ghee
  • 2 tablespoons biryani masala
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 bunch fresh mint leaves
  • 1 bunch fresh coriander leaves
  • 4-5 green chilies, chopped
  • Whole spices (bay leaves, cardamom, cinnamon, cloves)
  • Salt to taste
    For Marination:
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste

Ramen

Instructions:
1. Prepare the Broth:
In a large pot, combine chicken broth, water, chicken breasts, onion, carrots, celery, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, mirin, salt, and pepper.
Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for about 20-30 minutes until the chicken is cooked through and the flavors meld.
2. Cook the Chicken:
Remove the chicken breasts from the broth and shred them using two forks. Set aside.
3. Strain the Broth:
Strain the broth to remove the solids, leaving a clear and flavorful broth. Discard the solids.
4. Cook the Ramen Noodles:
Cook the ramen noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and rinse under cold water to stop the cooking process.
5. Assemble the Ramen:
Divide the cooked ramen noodles among serving bowls.
Pour the hot broth over the noodles.
Add shredded chicken, chopped bok choy, and sliced green onions.
6. Optional Toppings:
Drizzle with sesame oil for extra flavor.
Top with soft-boiled eggs, sliced nori, and red pepper flakes if desired.
7. Serve:
Serve the chicken ramen hot and enjoy!
Feel free to customize your ramen with additional toppings like mushrooms, bean sprouts, or any other vegetables you enjoy. Adjust the seasoning to your taste, and you'll have a delicious bowl of homemade chicken ramen.

    ingridients :
    For the Broth :
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 onion, halved
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, sliced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons mirin (sweet rice wine) or rice vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
    For the Ramen:
  • 4 packs of ramen noodles (discard the seasoning packets)
  • Bok choy, chopped
  • Green onions, sliced
  • Soft-boiled eggs (optional)
  • Sesame oil (for drizzling)
  • Nori (seaweed sheets), sliced (optional)
  • Red pepper flakes (optional)

Pani Puri

Instructions:
Making the Puris:
1. Mix semolina and all-purpose flour in a bowl.
2. Add water gradually and knead into a stiff but smooth dough.
3. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
4. Take small portions of the dough and roll them into small balls.
5. Roll out each ball into a small disc (approximately 2 inches in diameter).
6. Heat oil in a pan for deep frying. Fry the rolled discs until they puff up and turn golden brown.
7. Remove the puris from the oil and let them cool.
Making the Pani (Spiced Water):
1. In a blender, combine mint leaves, coriander leaves, green chilies, and ginger. Blend into a smooth paste.
2. Strain the paste to extract the juice. You can use a fine mesh strainer or cheesecloth.
3. In a bowl, mix the mint-coriander juice with cold water.
4. Add roasted cumin powder, black salt, chaat masala, lemon juice, and regular salt to the water. Adjust the quantities to your taste.
5. Chill the pani in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes to let the flavors meld.
Assembling the Pani Puri:
1. Carefully make a small hole in the center of each puri.
2. Stuff each puri with a small amount of mashed potatoes and boiled chickpeas.
3. Optionally, add chopped onions, tamarind chutney, and boondi.
4. Dip each stuffed puri into the chilled pani just before eating.
5. Serve immediately and enjoy the burst of flavors!
Pani Puri is best enjoyed fresh and is a fun and interactive way to savor the diverse flavors of Indian street food. Adjust the spice levels and ingredients according to your preferences.

    For the Puris :
  • 1 cup semolina (sooji)
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • Water for kneading
  • Oil for frying
    For the Pani (Spiced Water) :
  • 1 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves
  • 2-3 green chilies
    (adjust to taste)
  • 1-inch piece of ginger
  • 1 small lemon (juiced)
  • 1 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon black salt
  • 1 teaspoon chaat masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups cold water
    For the Filling:
  • 1 cup boiled and mashed potatoes
  • 1 cup boiled black chickpeas
  • Tamarind chutney (sweet and tangy tamarind sauce)
  • Chopped onions
  • Sev (thin gram flour noodles)
  • Optional: Boondi (tiny fried chickpea flour balls)

Chocolate Cake

Instructions:
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.
In a large bowl, sift together the flour, cocoa powder, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
Add eggs, milk, oil, and vanilla extract to the dry ingredients. Mix until well combined.
Gradually add boiling water to the batter, mixing well. The batter will be thin, but that's okay.
Pour the batter evenly into the prepared pans.
Bake in the preheated oven for 30 to 35 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
Allow the cakes to cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.
For the Chocolate Frosting:
In a large bowl, beat the softened butter until creamy.
Gradually add the powdered sugar and cocoa powder, alternating with milk. Beat until smooth and creamy.
Mix in the vanilla extract and a pinch of salt.
Assembly:
Once the cakes are completely cool, spread a layer of chocolate frosting on top of one cake layer.
Place the second cake layer on top and frost the top and sides of the entire cake.
You can decorate the cake with additional frosting, chocolate shavings, or other toppings of your choice.
Slice and enjoy your homemade chocolate cake!
Feel free to customize the recipe to your liking, and have fun with the decoration!

    powder
  • 1 and 1/2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
